Garage Conversion to ADU: Cost in the Bay Area (2026)

A Bay Area garage conversion to ADU typically costs $120K-$250K, or about $180-$380 per square foot, in 2026.

Converting a garage into a legal accessory dwelling unit is the single cheapest way to add a rentable, permitted living space to a Bay Area property, because you start with a foundation, a roof and four walls already in place. In 2026 a typical garage conversion to ADU in San Jose and Santa Clara County costs $120,000 to $250,000, or roughly $180 to $380 per square foot. This guide breaks the number down by garage type, walks through the permit fees and timeline, and explains the California rules that make conversions uniquely fast and flexible. For a full picture of the process, our garage conversion service page covers design through construction.

How much does it cost to convert a garage to an ADU in the Bay Area?

Expect to spend about $120,000 to $250,000 for a permitted garage-to-ADU conversion in the Bay Area in 2026. The broader market runs $150K to $400K, and higher-finish Silicon Valley and Los Gatos projects often land in the $225K-$300K range, but a straightforward San Jose conversion commonly finishes at $120K-$150K. The reason conversions beat a ground-up build is simple: a new detached ADU of the same size runs $250K-$350K because you pay for a new foundation, framing and roof that the garage already provides.

The number that matters is total delivered cost, not just construction. Design, engineering, permits, utility connections and finishes all factor in. For a deeper cost breakdown of ADUs generally, see our ADU cost guide for San Jose.

What is the cost per square foot for a garage conversion ADU?

Garage conversions run about $180 to $380 per square foot in the Bay Area, and where you land depends heavily on the existing structure. A detached garage with a sound slab and framing can come in near $180/sq ft. An attached garage that needs structural upgrades, a shared firewall and new utilities can reach $350-$380/sq ft. The typical band is $200-$400+ per square foot.

Smaller units carry a higher per-foot cost because the expensive rooms, kitchen and bathroom, are fixed costs spread over fewer square feet. A 400 sq ft studio therefore prices higher per foot than a 700 sq ft one-bedroom, even though the total is lower.

Attached vs. detached garage conversion: how does the cost differ?

An attached conversion often costs more per square foot than a detached one because it shares a wall with the main house, which triggers fire-separation, and utility runs are more tangled. A detached garage is essentially a small standalone building, so it converts cleanly. The table below shows typical 2026 Bay Area pricing by scenario.

What drives garage conversion ADU cost the most?

Four line items move the budget more than anything else, and together they explain most of the swing between a $120K and a $250K project:

  • Structural and foundation work — a thin garage slab often needs upgrading for habitable loads, and attached garages may need new footings or shear walls. This alone can add $15K-$40K.
  • New utilities — a dedicated electrical subpanel, plumbing, a sewer lateral connection and gas are frequently the biggest surprise. Trenching to the street sewer is expensive if the garage sits far from existing lines.
  • Adding square footage — the moment you extend past the original footprint you leave conversion territory, pay ground-up prices on the new area, and trigger a 4-ft side and rear setback.
  • Finish level — a rental-grade studio and a designer in-law suite can differ by $100/sq ft on cabinets, flooring, tile and appliances alone.

Other drivers include HVAC (a ductless mini-split is standard), egress windows for the bedroom, insulation to current energy code, and the kitchen and bathroom builds. Our ADU design-build service prices all of these transparently before construction starts.

How much are ADU permits and fees in San Jose and Santa Clara County?

San Jose is one of the cheapest permitting jurisdictions in the Bay Area, with building permit and plan-check fees of roughly $5,000 to $12,000. Add utility connection and other soft costs and the broader Bay Area range is $8,000-$18,000. The bigger savings come from waived impact fees: under SB 13 (2019, Gov. Code §65852.2), ADUs under 750 sq ft pay no impact fees, and school and parkland fees do not apply. SB 543 (2026) restates and expands that exemption for the current year, waiving development, connection and capacity charges on ADUs of 750 sq ft or less, with school developer fees also waived below 500 sq ft. Since most garage conversions fall under 750 sq ft, this waiver saves many homeowners $10K-$30K.

SB 543 also tightens the clock on cities: an agency must deem your application complete within 15 business days or it is automatically deemed complete. For the full local process, see our permitting service and our Santa Clara County ADU permit guide. Homeowners in the county's largest city should also read our San Jose ADU page.

How long does a garage conversion to ADU take?

Plan on 6 to 10 months from first conversation to Certificate of Occupancy. Construction itself is usually only 3 to 4 months; design, engineering and permitting fill the rest. California law helps here: ADU applications get ministerial approval (no discretionary hearing or neighbor review), and the city must act within 60 days of a complete submittal. Combined with the 15-business-day completeness rule under SB 543, a well-prepared package moves through review far faster than a typical remodel permit.

Is there a size limit for a garage conversion ADU in California?

No. California law requires ministerial approval of at least one ADU converted from existing space, such as a garage or accessory structure, with no maximum size limit on the conversion. This holds even if your lot is already maxed out on floor-area ratio or the garage encroaches into a setback. As long as you keep the existing footprint, no new setbacks apply, even if the garage sits on or near the property line. Extend beyond that footprint and a 4-ft side and rear setback kicks in on the new area. For the full size rulebook, see our California ADU size rules guide.

Do I have to replace my garage parking?

No. Under current state law (AB 68 and successors), cities cannot require replacement parking when you convert a garage to an ADU. You are free to lose the covered parking spot without providing a new one, which removes what used to be a common project killer, especially on smaller Silicon Valley lots where fitting a replacement space was impossible. This one rule is a big reason garage conversions pencil out so well compared with other ADU types.

Does a garage conversion ADU add value, and is it worth it?

A permitted garage conversion typically adds 15-25% to property value, roughly $100K-$250K in most Bay Area markets, and homeowners usually recoup 60-80% of project cost at resale. On the rental side, at around $2,300/month a Bay Area ADU often breaks even in 4-6 years. Two 2026 laws sweeten the math further: AB 976 (2023/2024) permanently banned owner-occupancy requirements for standard ADUs, so you can rent both the main house and the ADU, and AB 1033 (2023) lets cities opt in to allow ADUs to be sold separately as condos, San Jose being the first to do so (first sale about $530K in August 2025). Note that AB 1154 (2026) reinstated owner-occupancy for junior ADUs that share a bathroom with the main home, so a JADU is treated differently than a full garage conversion. If you are curious about selling separately, see our AB 1033 condo-sale guide.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to convert a garage into an ADU in the Bay Area?

About $120,000 to $250,000 in 2026, or roughly $180-$380 per square foot. A straightforward San Jose conversion often finishes near $120K-$150K, while higher-finish Silicon Valley projects can reach $225K-$300K.

Is a garage conversion cheaper than building a new detached ADU?

Yes. A garage conversion runs $120K-$250K because the foundation, walls and roof already exist. A new detached ADU of similar size costs $250K-$350K since you pay for all-new structure.

Are there permit or impact fees for a garage conversion ADU in San Jose?

San Jose building and plan-check fees run about $5,000-$12,000. Under SB 13 and SB 543, ADUs under 750 sq ft pay no impact fees, and school and parkland fees do not apply, which most garage conversions qualify for.

Is there a maximum size for a garage conversion ADU in California?

No. State law requires ministerial approval of an ADU converted from existing space with no maximum size limit, even if the lot is maxed on floor-area ratio or the garage sits in a setback, as long as you keep the existing footprint.

Do I have to replace parking when I convert my garage to an ADU?

No. Under current California law (AB 68 and successors), cities cannot require you to provide replacement parking when you convert a garage into an ADU.

How long does a garage-to-ADU conversion take?

Typically 6-10 months end to end, with 3-4 months of construction. ADUs receive ministerial approval, and the city must act within 60 days of a complete application and deem it complete within 15 business days under SB 543.

Key Takeaways

  • A Bay Area garage conversion to ADU typically costs $120K-$250K, or about $180-$380 per square foot, in 2026.
  • Converting an existing garage has no maximum size limit under California law, and cities cannot force you to replace the lost parking.
  • San Jose building permit and plan-check fees run roughly $5,000-$12,000, and impact fees are waived for ADUs under 750 sq ft (SB 13 / SB 543).
  • Plan on 6-10 months end to end, with state law requiring ministerial approval within 60 days of a complete application.
