Once you have decided to build an accessory dwelling unit (ADU), the next big fork is how to build it: a prefab (factory-built) ADU or a custom, site-built ADU. Prefab units are manufactured in a factory and delivered to your lot, promising speed and a lower headline price. Custom ADUs are designed and framed on your property, giving you total control over layout, finishes and how the unit fits your land. In the Bay Area, prefab typically runs $150K-$300K all-in over 4-8 months, while custom runs $250K-$550K over 8-14 months - but the real decision is driven by your lot, not just the sticker price. This guide compares the two on cost, timeline, site suitability, financing and resale so you can choose the path your property is actually built for.

Is a prefab ADU actually cheaper than a custom ADU in the Bay Area?

Not as much as the brochure suggests. A prefab ADU often lands at $150K-$300K all-in versus $250K-$550K for custom, but the factory unit is only part of the bill. In the Bay Area, site work - foundation, utility trenching and hookups, and crane delivery - adds roughly $30K-$50K (sometimes up to $80K on tight lots), which erodes most of prefab's headline savings. Once permitting, site prep and finish work are added, the two paths frequently land in a similar total range. So "prefab is always cheaper" is a myth worth retiring before you sign anything.

It also helps to know the three prefab sub-types, because they price and behave differently:

  • Modular - large factory-built sections assembled on-site; closest to traditional site-built quality.
  • Panelized - flat-pack walls, panels and trusses shipped and stood up on-site; more design flexibility than a fixed module.
  • Fully-assembled - a single complete unit craned into place; fastest, but limited in size by transport and crane reach.

How much faster is a prefab ADU - and does the timeline hold on a real lot?

Prefab is genuinely faster: expect 4-8 months versus 8-14 months for custom. The reason is parallel scheduling - the factory frames your unit indoors while your crew pours the foundation and runs utilities on-site, and the factory portion never loses days to Bay Area rain. A fully-assembled unit can be set in a single crane day.

That speed holds only when the site cooperates. Permitting still runs on the same clock for both paths - Santa Clara County is roughly 4-12 weeks application-to-approval and San Jose about 2-6 months submission-to-issuance, with state law requiring the city to approve or deny within 60 days of a complete application. And prefab's factory savings can evaporate if crane access is blocked, if the module needs an oversized-load transport permit, or if utility trenching hits complications. On a clean, flat lot prefab's timeline advantage is real; on a constrained lot, custom's continuous on-site sequence can actually be more predictable.

Which ADU type works on my lot - flat vs. hillside or narrow?

Your lot is the single biggest deciding factor. Prefab needs a flat lot with driveway or street access wide enough for a crane and an oversized delivery truck; custom site-built handles any lot - sloped, narrow, or shaped by awkward setbacks. If a crane cannot reach your building pad, a fully-assembled or modular unit simply is not an option, and even panelized delivery gets expensive.

This is exactly where Bay Area geography matters. Hillside lots in Los Gatos and Saratoga, narrow lots in Palo Alto, and the deep, irregular parcels of older San Jose neighborhoods often rule prefab out or make custom the smarter fit. Custom also lets you shape the unit around a tree, a slope or a 4-foot side-and-rear setback to squeeze out every usable square foot. A quick feasibility walk of your lot - checking crane paths, slope and setbacks - tells you within an hour which path is viable. If your lot has an existing garage, a garage conversion is a third route worth pricing; see our garage conversion ADU cost guide for numbers.

How much does an ADU cost in San Jose and Santa Clara County in 2026?

Plan on roughly $150K-$300K for prefab and $250K-$550K for custom all-in, plus $5K-$15K in San Jose permit fees depending on scope. Detached ADUs are allowed up to 1,200 sq ft; many Santa Clara County cities now permit up to 1,200 sq ft for attached units too, while a JADU is capped at 500 sq ft within the existing home. Standard detached setbacks are 4 ft from side and rear lines, and garage conversions that keep the existing footprint need no added setback.

What are the 2026 California ADU laws I need to know?

Four state laws shape every ADU project in 2026, prefab or custom alike:

  • SB 13 (2019): No impact fees on ADUs under 750 sq ft. Units 750 sq ft or larger are charged proportionally, based on the ratio of ADU-to-primary-home square footage - the core "build under 750 to skip fees" lever.
  • AB 976 (2023): Made the owner-occupancy exemption permanent. You do not have to live on-site to rent a standard ADU - you can rent both the main home and the ADU.
  • AB 1033 (2023): Lets cities opt in to allow an ADU to be sold separately as a condominium, turning it into an independently saleable asset. See our guide to selling an ADU separately under AB 1033.
  • AB 1154 (effective Jan 1, 2026): Narrowed JADU owner-occupancy - the requirement now applies only when a JADU shares a bathroom with the main house. A JADU with its own bathroom carries no owner-occupancy rule.

One bonus for 2026: SB 543 sets a 15-day completeness determination and exempts ADUs and JADUs under 500 sq ft from school impact fees. None of these laws favor prefab or custom over the other - the build method does not change your legal footing.

Can I rent out or sell my prefab or custom ADU separately?

Yes on renting, and sometimes on selling. Thanks to AB 976, you can rent both your primary home and your ADU with no owner-occupancy requirement - a well-built Bay Area unit generates $18K-$54K+ a year. Selling the ADU on its own is possible only in cities that have opted into AB 1033, which lets you convey it as a condominium; San Jose is among the early adopters. The build method has no bearing on either right - a prefab and a custom ADU carry identical rental and resale rights. What differs is buyer perception at resale, covered below.

How do I finance a prefab vs. custom ADU?

Most Bay Area homeowners tap home equity, and the mechanics slightly favor custom's phased schedule. A HELOC is the most popular route (about 48% of projects) because it preserves your existing first-mortgage rate and its revolving structure matches phased construction draws - early-April 2026 California credit-union rates ran as low as 6.50%-6.75% for well-qualified borrowers. A cash-out refinance (about 32% of projects) sits around 6.5%, and a fixed home equity loan runs roughly 8.0%-10.5% over 5-20 years.

The wrinkle for prefab: some lenders treat modular units differently and phased draws can be harder to structure, because a large factory deposit comes due before there is on-site collateral. Custom's continuous, milestone-based schedule maps more cleanly to construction draws. Do not overlook the CalHFA ADU Grant, still active in 2026, which offers up to $40,000 toward pre-development and non-recurring closing costs - it applies to either build method.

Does an ADU add resale value - and does prefab vs. custom matter to buyers?

An ADU adds real value either way: figure roughly 20-35% of construction cost back into your property value, or $200K to $500K+ in dollar terms, with some research showing ADU homes selling at about a 35% premium. The build method makes only a modest difference. Custom's bespoke fit tends to maximize usable square footage and command a premium, while prefab occasionally draws slightly lower buyer perception around the word "manufactured" - though a quality modular unit is largely indistinguishable once finished. For most owners, the value gap between prefab and custom at resale is small compared with the value of having a permitted, rentable ADU at all.

Prefab vs custom ADU: which should you build?

Decide with your lot and your goals. Choose prefab if you have a flat lot with driveway or crane access, you want speed, and a standard catalog layout fits your needs - it is the faster, often lower-friction path at $150K-$300K in 4-8 months. Choose custom if your lot is sloped, narrow or oddly shaped, you want to maximize usable square footage, or you need a specific design - common realities for Los Gatos hillsides, Palo Alto narrow lots and older San Jose neighborhoods. Frequently Asked Questions

Is a prefab ADU cheaper than a custom ADU in the Bay Area?

Often less than you'd expect. Prefab runs about $150K-$300K all-in versus $250K-$550K for custom, but Bay Area site work - foundation, utilities and crane delivery - adds $30K-$50K (up to $80K on tight lots), which erodes prefab's headline savings. After permitting and finish work, the two paths frequently land in a similar total range.

How much faster is a prefab ADU than a custom one?

Prefab typically finishes in 4-8 months versus 8-14 months for custom, because the factory frames the unit while your crew preps the site, with no weather delays on the structure. That advantage holds on a flat, crane-accessible lot; on constrained sites, blocked crane access or oversized-load transport can erase it.

Can a prefab ADU go on a hillside or narrow lot?

Usually not easily. Prefab needs a flat lot with driveway or street access wide enough for a crane and an oversized delivery truck. Sloped Los Gatos hillsides, narrow Palo Alto lots and irregular older San Jose parcels often rule prefab out and make custom site-built - which can be shaped around slope and setbacks - the better fit.

Do prefab and custom ADUs have the same rental and resale rights?

Yes. The build method has no bearing on your legal rights. Under AB 976 you can rent both your main home and either type of ADU with no owner-occupancy requirement, and in cities that opt into AB 1033 you can sell either type separately as a condominium. Buyer perception of custom is sometimes slightly higher at resale, but both add value.

Do impact fees apply to prefab or custom ADUs?

Under SB 13, no ADU under 750 sq ft is charged impact fees, regardless of whether it is prefab or custom. Units 750 sq ft or larger are charged proportionally, based on the ratio of ADU-to-primary-home square footage. San Jose permit fees run about $5K-$15K depending on scope for either method.

Which should I choose, prefab or custom?

Choose prefab if you have a flat lot with crane access, want speed, and a standard layout fits ($150K-$300K, 4-8 months). Choose custom if your lot is sloped, narrow or oddly shaped, you want to maximize usable square footage, or you need a specific design ($250K-$550K, 8-14 months).
