FAQ

Common Questions (Real Intake Topics)

Cost and Pricing

How much does a custom home cost in the Bay Area?

Typical construction ranges are about $250 to $500+ per square foot depending on design complexity, material selections, and site conditions.

How much does a kitchen remodel cost in the Bay Area?

Actual cost depends on layout changes, cabinetry scope, appliance tier, and finish level. A line-item estimate should be used instead of rough allowances.

How do you reduce change-order risk and budget surprises?

By finalizing decisions early through 3D validation, line-item pricing, and written approval gates before construction starts, risk is significantly reduced.

Our Process and Timeline

What is included in the design phase?

Design phase scope typically includes full 2D planning, photorealistic 3D walkthroughs, finish selections, and permit-ready drawings before build kickoff.

How long does it take to build a custom home?

Timeline depends on scope, approvals, and site constraints. A reliable schedule is created only after design, documentation, and permitting strategy are defined.

Do you handle permits?

Yes. Permit coordination is part of the core workflow so documentation, code compliance, and agency submissions stay aligned with the design intent.

Getting Started

Do I need a permit for an ADU?

In most cases yes. ADUs generally require planning and building permits, and requirements vary by city and property conditions.

Can I rent out my ADU?

Many Bay Area jurisdictions allow ADU rental, but local ordinances and permitting conditions should be reviewed before planning the unit program.

Do I need to move out during a whole-house remodel?

It depends on project phasing, safety, and utility shutoffs. For major structural or full-scope interior work, temporary relocation is often recommended.
